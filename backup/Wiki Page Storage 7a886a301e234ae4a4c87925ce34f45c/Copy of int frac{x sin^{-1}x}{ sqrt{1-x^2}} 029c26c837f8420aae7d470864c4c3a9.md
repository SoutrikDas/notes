$$\int \frac{x \sin^{-1}x}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}$$

substituting $t=\sin^{-1}x$   we get 

- $x=\sin t$

$$\int t\sin t  \quad dt$$

Using By parts (DI method)

$$\begin{matrix}
& D&I \\
& t& \sin t \\
\hdashline \\
-& 1& \cos t \\
+ &0 &-\sin t
\end{matrix}$$

Answer is 

$$t\cos t+\sin t+ c$$

$$\left(\sin^{-1}x\times \sqrt{1-x^2}\right)+x+ c$$
